Executive Summary

This blueprint outlines a knowledge-graph-first, explainable AI platform for continuous vendor risk intelligence targeting Indian financial institutions. The core innovation is source-level evidence anchoring—every risk score change must be traceable to verifiable raw data, satisfying regulatory mandates (RBI FREE-AI) and operational trust requirements.

The platform combines event-driven + periodic monitoring across six risk dimensions (cybersecurity to operational stability), graph-based risk propagation for nth-party and concentration risks, and hybrid AI (ensemble learning + LLMs) with SHAP/LIME explanations. For hackathon feasibility, the prototype focuses on cybersecurity + operational dimensions with a live explainability dashboard, while the production roadmap expands to full regulatory integration and enterprise system connectivity.

1. The Core Problem & Value Proposition

1.1 The Explainability Imperative

The foundational requirement for this vendor risk intelligence platform stems from a critical insight that has emerged from both regulatory pressure and operational necessity: risk scores without transparent, verifiable provenance are fundamentally untrustworthy regardless of their predictive accuracy. The problem statement explicitly declares that "the system must be explainable: showing why a risk score changed, with source-level evidence, so a human can verify before acting. Black-box scores will not be trusted, no matter how accurate."

Source-Level Evidence Requirement

The source-level evidence requirement demands that every risk score computation be traceable to specific, identifiable data points from verifiable sources. This goes far beyond generic explanations like "financial health deteriorated" or "negative news detected."

  • Specific MCA filing showing a 40% drop in quarterly revenue
  • Exact SecurityScorecard vulnerability report identifying three critical unpatched CVEs
  • Court case docket number referencing a fraud allegation
  • Precise news article timestamp and excerpt mentioning a data breach

Research on hybrid AI-based dynamic risk assessment frameworks emphasizes that "trusting model-driven decisions requires them to be transparent as well as auditable, thus XAI techniques like feature contribution and feature correlation are integrated with risk management to maintain decision-driven transparency" [187]. These explainability tools help verify the key features and their interactions that drive predictive algorithms, thereby allowing more precise control implementation and human verification.
